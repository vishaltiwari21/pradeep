About Agroforestry

Agroforestry is a land use management system that combines trees and shrubs with crops and/or livestock in a single system. It has been used for centuries by indigenous people around the world, and is gaining popularity as a sustainable and resilient farming practice.

Benefits of Agroforestry

  • Increases biodiversity and ecosystem services
  • Improves soil health and fertility
  • Reduces erosion and water runoff
  • Diversifies farm income
  • Enhances climate change resilience

Examples of Agroforestry

There are many different types of agroforestry systems, including:

  • Alley cropping
  • Forest farming
  • Silvopasture
  • Windbreaks
